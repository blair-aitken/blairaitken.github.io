Hi, my name is

BLAIR AITKEN

I'm a Postdoctoral Research Fellow dedicated to improving road safety through high-quality research. Currently, I'm focused on developing new methods for real-time impairment detection through ocular assessment.

01. About Me

Over the years, I've had the opportunity to collaborate with leading experts, and am currently a member of Swinburne University's Drugs and Driving Research Unit as well as the ICADTS Driver Monitoring System Working Group.

My current research is centered around the development of next-generation driver monitoring systems that can detect driving impairment and crash risk in real-time. These systems identify distinct visual scanning patterns linked to impaired driving and promptly alert the driver to prevent potential road accidents.

I have experience in various analytical tools and psychometric assessments, such Forum8's UC-win/Road driving simulation software, cognitive assessment tools like CANTAB AND DRUID, as well as programming languages R and Python, among others.

02. Current Project

My current research is focused on the effect of alcohol on gaze behaviour during simulated driving. This project, in conjunction with industry leaders Seeing Machines, intends to adapt and create eye-tracking technologies capable of indexing and monitoring intoxicated drivers in real-time, providing immediate alerts upon detection of driving impairment. This technology represents a significant step forward in our efforts to improve road safety, particularly in an era where technology is increasingly intersecting with road safety.

My responsibilities include, but are not limited to, handling recruitment, data collection, management and analysis, and overall project administration.
